# Sanctions Screening Protocol

This is a reference for detailed sanctions screening. In most geopolitical reports, sanctions screening should be flagged as a **recommended follow-up action** requiring legal counsel — not performed inline during the report, as it consumes too many turns and requires specialized databases.

## When to Flag (Not Perform) Sanctions Screening

Flag sanctions as a data gap and recommend dedicated screening when:
- The geopolitical event involves sanctioned countries (Iran, Russia, North Korea, Syria, Cuba, etc.)
- Portfolio companies have suppliers, customers, or operations in sanctioned jurisdictions
- New sanctions are announced or imminent

## Recommended Flag Language

> **Sanctions Exposure: [INSUFFICIENT DATA — LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]**
> Portfolio companies with [Country] exposure should undergo dedicated OFAC/EU/UK sanctions screening.
> Recommended: Engage sanctions counsel to screen all entities (companies, subsidiaries, directors, key vendors) against OFAC SDN, EU Consolidated List, UK OFSI, and UN SC lists.
> Estimated cost: $25K-$75K depending on portfolio size. Timeline: 2-4 weeks.
> MOIC relevance: Sanctions violations carry penalties of $250K-$10M+ per violation and potential forced divestiture.

## Screening Lists (for reference)

| List | Jurisdiction | Administrator |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| SDN (Specially Designated Nationals) | US | OFAC |
| Sectoral Sanctions (SSI) | US | OFAC |
| Entity List / Denied Persons | US | BIS |
| EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions | EU | European Commission |
| UK OFSI Consolidated List | UK | HM Treasury |
| UN Security Council Consolidated List | International | UN |

## 50% Ownership Rule

Under OFAC guidance, entities 50%+ owned by an SDN are themselves blocked, even if not individually listed. This is critical for PE portfolios with majority ownership positions.
